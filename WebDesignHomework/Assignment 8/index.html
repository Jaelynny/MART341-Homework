Men Categories

Fancy

What is fancy?

A war dance first meant to be used as participaton in battle. In modern times, the footwork has changed to a basic simple step while dancing around the Drum and a contest step with fast and intricate footwork combined with a spinning up and down movement of the body.
Chicken

What is Chicken?

Chicken dance is one of the oldest and most symbolic dances known to many Indigenous Nations throughout North America. Based on the prairie chicken it showcases incredible synchronization and artistry. The dance meaning is based off the prairie birds mating dance, lowering their heads, tails facing forwrds, stamping their feet and lowering the wings to touch the ground.
Traditonal

What is Traditonal?

Another form of warriors dance meant to represent leaving leave one's track. Its to tell a story while making use of the stick and feather in the dancers hand.
Grass

What is Grass?

Grass dancing is believed to have came from young boys tying grass on their outfits. Before a dance could be held on the prairie the grass had to be stomped down. This is where many of the movements are believed to come from. Afterward, the dancers would tie the grass to their outfits. Many believe that the Omaha tribe originated the dance in their warrior societies.

Women Categories

Jingle

What is Jingle?

Jingle is a representation of healing powers, that whoever wore the dress would heal the child when danced in. The dance gets it name from the metal cones that make an unique sound when danced in a rythym. In recent Powwows the dance is used for healing families of the MMIW and MMIP.
Shawl

What is Shawl?

The dance mimic butterflies in flight, and the dance style is quite graceful and light. Another term used for this dance is Fancy and its the most athletic dance in womens.
Buckskin

What is Buckskin?

Commonly like men traditional, the dance is of elegance and grace.Ladies' Buckskin Dancing is slow, and poised. Circling the drum, they bob to the beat of the drum, letting the long fringe on their sleeves sway in time. They carry a beaded purse, swinging it as well, and a shawl, folded on the arm, swaying likewise.

Powwow Etiquette

You should know several guidelines as a courtesy to the powwow participants. Powwows are fun events, but they are also sacred events.

  • Please Respect Elders at all times.
  • Elders are to be served FIRST at mealtimes.
  • Do not touch dancers' regalia or jewelry
  • Walk around, not through the dance circle
  • PLease do not dance in the arena until announcer invites.
  • Do not pick up fallen feathers, only a designated person can do that.
  • Every nationality is invited, please no judgement.
